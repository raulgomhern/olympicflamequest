MILETUS: RESPECT TO THE OTHERS

In the 20th century, respect was not
always a value that was upheld.
But after the First World War,in 1936,
there was an extraordinary example
that played a part in changing that:
Jesse Owens, a black man, won four gold medals
at the Olympic Games in Nazi Germany,
defying the racist regime.

Please, do not judge people by
the colour of their skin
respect and help each other.

CORINTH: FAIR PLAY

Although, there are people who want to go faster then cheat in sports,
this practice is not true to the Olympic spirit.
When you cheat, you do not show how good you are.
Instead, it shows you are not confident about yourself.

As the example of the city of Corinth shows,
please, do not cheat yourself and the others,
make an effort and practise.
This is how you become your best self.

ATHENS: BALANCE BETWEEN BODY, WILL AND MIND

Competing with others is hard work for your muscles and your mind.
Maintaining a healthy balance between body and mind is essential for going far.
In Ancient Greece, Muses were protectors of Arts.
For this reason, artistic disciplines
were included in Ancient Olympic Games to honour these goddesses.
Singing with a harp, playing the flute and reciting a text were serious competitions.
Later, from 1912 to 1952, in Modern Olympic Games,
sports disciplines were combined with artistic disciplines.
Do not stop practising an artistic discipline,
because it helps to maintain a proper balance in your life.
